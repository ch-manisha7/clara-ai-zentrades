[Sales Rep]: Thanks for taking the time today. Can you tell me a bit about your business?

[Client]: On a busy day maybe 40-50 calls. A lot of them are scheduling calls for routine inspections, but we also get emergency calls. When a sprinkler line bursts or a fire alarm goes off, those are time-sensitive situations.

[Sales Rep]: And when do those emergency calls come in?

[Client]: Honestly all times. We're officially open 8am to 5pm Monday through Friday, Eastern time. But emergencies don't follow business hours, so we have an on-call technician who handles after-hours emergencies.

[Sales Rep]: How does that currently work? The after-hours routing?

[Client]: Right now our office manager manually forwards the main line to the on-call tech's cell phone after 5. It's clunky. Sometimes she forgets, or there's a gap. We've missed some emergency calls that way.

[Sales Rep]: What would you consider a true emergency for your business?

[Client]: A sprinkler system that's actively flowing water, a fire alarm that's triggered and won't reset, or anything that poses an immediate life-safety risk. Inspection scheduling, equipment questions, those can wait until business hours.

[Sales Rep]: And where are you based out of?
